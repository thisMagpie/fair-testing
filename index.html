Introduction to This Project

The project aim is to answer the question of whether university examination procedures are fair by testing the accepted hypothesis set put by the Edinburgh University Taught Assessment Regulations against physical exam data of students in the School of Physics and Astronomy at the University to see if the university model works enough to support the assertion that a 2% standard deviation on a student classification has any reliable, reproducible basis in reality.

Introduction to This Page

This page provides a general summary of the relevant points noted during email and meeting discussions between Prof. Graeme Ackland and Magdalen Berns about the project.

The site also also provides the reference material compiled over the course of the project and links to the relevant pages which combine to form the project lab book in digital form.

Initial Test Parameters

Gaussian One: Student Ability

This Gaussian assumes the student is capable of achieving 60% within a standard deviation of 22.6%. The error estimate made is not fully justified. There is a real possibility the student will fail so this estimate for the standard deviation is likely to be too low when compared with the actual physical data available.

However, the system is trialing the exam scores of a population of students from the Edinburgh University School of Physics and Astronomy, which is Ranked No. 17 in the world and follows a syllabus which is standardized to conform to the Institute of Physics criteria for accredited British Physics degrees hence it is expected that a University which only allows the very strongest physics applicants of the world onto to study will in turn find a greater number of student achieve the very highest grades of the country at the end of their degree.

To simplify matters. This Study will only look at those courses which are part of a physics degree accredited by the Institute of Physics. This will allow a qualitative assessment of the results in context of the county pass fail progression rate at the end of the study.

  • mean: 60%
  • std deviation 22.6%

Gaussian two: Test Difficulty

  • mean: 60%
  • std deviation 2%
